Spices

Spices serves a variety of Asian cuisines- Vietnamese, Thai, Indonesian and Indian. Consistent quality and generous portions. Every time, we get the chicken curry, naan bread, prawn cakes, and dessert platter. My comfort food.

Address: 109 Repulse Bay Road, Repulse Bay
Phone: 2292 2821

Bep Vietnamese Kitchen

One of my favourite cuisines! Their fresh, tasty and flavourful Vietnamese small dishes are filling but leave you feeling good.

Address: 9 Staunton Street, Lower Ground Floor (alley entrance), Central, Hong Kong
Phone: 2552 7533

TRi

Contemporary Balinese food by Repulse Bay beach. The interior decor captures the essence of Bali. Their "dadar gulung", Balinese pancake with coconut filling, was so good we ordered a second one!

Address: Shop 302, 3/F, Lobby C The Pulse, Repulse Bay
Phone: 2515 0577

Kaya

Korean BBQ restaurant that Koreans actually go to. "Meat meat meat on parade!"

Address: 9/F, Midtown Soundwill Plaza II, 1 Tang Lung Street, Causeway Bay
Phone: 2838 9550

Chachawan

One of the best Thai restaurants in HK. Inspired by Isaan food (in northern Thailand, not the typical Thai food you would think of), the dishes at Chachawan are super flavourful and fresh. Highly recommend.

Address: Chachawan 206 Hollywood Rd, Sheung Wan
Phone: 2549 0020

Brass Spoon

Popular Vietnamese eatery. Their beef pho is delicious!

Address: Shop B G/F 1-, 3 Moon St, Wan Chai
Phone: 2877 0898

Yardbird

Great vibe and awesome food! They serve small, yummy Japanese yakitori dishes. Get the sweet corn tempura, korean fried cauliflower, brussel sprouts, asparagus and onsen egg, chicken breast/ thigh yakitori, meatball yakitori, rice cakes, and chicken and egg rice.

Address: 33 Bridges St, Sheung Wan
Phone: 2547 9273

Saboten

Tucked in the basement of Henry House, Saboten is my to-go place for Japanese tonkatsu. Their pork loin and egg in clay pot is always on point.

Address: Lower Ground Floor, Henry House, 42 Yun Ping Road, Causeway Bay
Phone: 2895 4111

Little Bao

Little Bao's menu consists of small plates, with a highlight on Asian-styled mini sliders or "baos" (pork belly, chicken, fish tempura and vegetarian). The use fresh ingredients and growth hormones-free meat. Their dessert bao, a deep fried bun with matcha ice cream and condensed milk is to die for.

Address: G/F, 66 Staunton Street, Central, Central, Hong Kong
Phone: 2194 0202
